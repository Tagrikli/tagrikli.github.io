Yearbook Website

01/12/2025

I was in my last year in one of the public high schools in Istanbul, Yesilkoy Anadolu Lisesi, where some of my sweetest memories were formed.

Even though we had internet haha, we were still writing our yearbook entries using a text editor and passing them to the teacher with USB sticks.
I wasn't aware of any website that eased this process, and even though I should have been studying for my university exam, I thought this would be a good project, and I thought I could manage them both.

So in a week or so, I set up Apache on a Windows machine that resided in my home, configured the router to forward the required port, got a free dynamic DNS domain, and wrote several PHP pages to make it work.

The Workflow

There was no registration. I don't remember how I got all of the students' names, but I just hard-coded everyone's name in a big select tag. Then I somehow generated unique passwords for each student and distributed them physically.
So what they needed to do was only:
  • Enter the website.
  • Pick themselves and the person they wanted to write to.
  • Write their yearbook entry and press the Send/Update button.
Their entries were sent to the computer and stored in a directory.
I had a few maintenance issues. Whenever something happened to the computer, I needed to be there to fix it in order to keep the website alive. It was a 24/7 service after all.
And whenever a new person needed to be added, or someone lost their password, I was there to modify the site and update their passwords.
So overall, it took some effort and time, but it was a nice thing to have, and I felt useful.
